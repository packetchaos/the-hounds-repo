GARMR

Tag Removal
Garmr Hound · Category: Ops
“Sweep out the tags you don't want.”

The gatekeeper hound — Garmr decides what leaves the estate, deliberately and on the record.

THE PROBLEM

Tag estates rot. Abandoned experiments, superseded schemes, tags whose owners left years ago — they pile up until nobody trusts what any tag means. And because tags drive scans, automation, and reporting, a rotten tag estate quietly corrupts everything built on it.

WHAT IT DOES

Garmr lists every tag in the estate and cleanly removes the ones you don't want. Removal is deliberate, reviewed, and logged — the stale and the abandoned get swept out without touching the tags that matter.

KEY CAPABILITIES

  • Full tag inventory — every tag and category in the estate laid out before you decide, so removals are informed choices rather than educated guesses about what might break.
  • Clean, targeted removal — retire exactly the tags you choose, with no collateral damage to the schemes that matter. Removals are permanent — which is exactly why each one is reviewed.
  • Removal policy for the Contract — hand Covenant a standing cleanup rule and hygiene runs on a schedule, so the estate stays clean without an annual purge project.

HOW IT WORKS

Garmr reads the tag estate from your Tenable data and proposes removals. Every removal is proposed, human-approved, and logged — removals are permanent, which is exactly why they're gated.

WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

  • Hygiene as a first-class function — most stacks treat tag cleanup as an afterthought; the pack gives it a dedicated hound.
  • Nothing vanishes silently — even deletions go through the same propose-approve-log gate as everything else.
  • Cleanup that keeps itself clean — as a Contract policy, Garmr's sweep recurs on schedule instead of waiting for the annual purge.

PROOF POINTS

  • In a reference environment of 268 assets, the pack's producers generate tag schemes across KEV months, identities, AI roles, certs, IoT devices, and EOL software — Garmr is what keeps that growing estate coherent instead of chaotic.

Illustrative demo-lab context — not a guarantee. Removals are permanent; review before you approve.

WORKS BETTER WITH

Garmr pairs with Sirius — structure laid down, clutter swept out — and feeds Covenant, where its removal policy becomes scheduled, auditable hygiene.

WHO IT'S FOR

Tag governance owners and security operations teams who need the tag estate to stay meaningful as it grows.

CALL TO ACTION

Put Garmr at the gate — nothing stale gets to stay.
