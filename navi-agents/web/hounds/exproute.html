ATLAS

Ownership Assignment
Atlas Hound · Category: Tagging / Ownership
“Every route has an owner.”

The Titan who gave his name to the atlas — the book of maps. Atlas maps every exposure route and filesystem path to the human who can actually fix it, so no finding is left without an owner.

THE PROBLEM

A vulnerability with no owner is a vulnerability nobody fixes. Findings pile up in a flat list of thousands, and the single hardest question in remediation goes unanswered: who owns this? Without ownership, tickets bounce, SLAs slip, and the same routes rot release after release.

WHAT IT DOES

Atlas pulls the org's users and groups (navi primary, Tenable MCP backup), then lets you describe in plain English who owns which exposure routes and filesystem paths. It tags every asset on a matched route or path with Owner : <group/user> — a persistent, gated tag — and tracks how much of the attack surface now has an owner.

KEY CAPABILITIES

  • Owner directory, two sources — pulls users, user-groups and agent-groups from navi's explore info first, falling back to the Tenable MCP, paginated and searchable so you assign to real principals, not free text.
  • Plain-English ownership mapping — “give the payment & billing routes to the VM Team, put /opt and nginx paths under Networking Systems” becomes a set of proposed Owner tags across every asset on those routes and paths.
  • Route- and path-aware tagging — resolves assets through the vuln_route and filesystem-path data, so an owner inherits every finding on their stack, not a hand-picked host list.
  • Coverage tracking — a live metric of how much of the attack surface has an owner assigned, so the blind spots — the un-owned routes — are impossible to hide.
  • Persistent, gated tags — Owner tags are stable classifications (no ephemeral churn); every write is proposed, human-approved, and logged in the Tagging log.

HOW IT WORKS

Atlas reads the local navi.db built from your Tenable data — the vuln_route table plus filesystem paths for the exposure map, and navi's live owner lists for the principals. Mappings you approve tag every asset on the matched route/path Owner : <group/user> via a persistent (no-remove) tag. All writes are gated and logged.

WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

  • Ownership, not just findings — most tools stop at “here's the vuln”; Atlas answers “here's who fixes it.”
  • Routes over hosts — one mapping covers a whole technology route, so five owner conversations can cover most of the estate.
  • Coverage as a first-class metric — un-owned surface is surfaced, not buried.
  • Gated, reviewable writes — Atlas proposes Owner tags; a human approves before anything lands.

PROOF POINTS

  • In a reference environment, Atlas collapses thousands of findings into a handful of technology routes — each mappable to one owning team in a single gated action.
  • Owner tags then flow to every downstream view — dashboards, the contract, and reporting all inherit accountability from one place.

Illustrative demo-lab context — not a guarantee. Ownership coverage depends on route/path data quality; un-owned routes are called out, not hidden.

WORKS BETTER WITH

Atlas pairs with Argus (custom-app discovery feeds route ownership), Mimir (software routes get technology owners), and the AI Contract (Owner tags become part of the reviewed, applied plan).

WHO IT'S FOR

Vulnerability management leads who need findings to route to a human; remediation owners who want their whole stack in one view; anyone whose SLA program dies without clear accountability.

CALL TO ACTION

Give Atlas the map — and put a name on every route.
