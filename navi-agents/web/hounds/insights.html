ON THE SCENT

Unknown-Unknowns Dashboard
The Pack's Trail · Category: Correlation
“The one page that shows what you're not seeing.”

Every hound's findings converge on one trail — the daily view of the exposure you didn't know you had.

THE PROBLEM

Each specialist agent answers its own question brilliantly — but a CISO doesn't want fifteen answers, they want one picture. Without a converging view, the most dangerous category of risk survives: the unknown-unknowns nobody thought to ask any single tool about.

WHAT IT DOES

On the Scent aggregates every agent's findings into a single “Exposure at a glance” page — a ranked view plus category tiles covering the whole pack's territory. Click any bar to drill into the Dashboard builder. It's the daily executive view: what the pack found, ranked by what matters, on one page.

KEY CAPABILITIES

  • Exposure-at-a-glance ranked view — every category of finding across the whole pack, ordered by significance on one screen. The morning read that replaces fifteen tool logins.
  • Category tiles — KEV, identity, crypto, AI, IoT, EOL and more, each tile a doorway into its specialist's full detail. Executive summary on top, evidence one click down.
  • Click-to-drill into the Dashboard builder — any bar becomes a working query, so “what's behind that number?” is answered by exploring, not by filing a request.
  • Unknown-unknowns framing — the page is organized around what you're not seeing, surfacing the risk categories nobody thought to ask any single tool about.

HOW IT WORKS

On the Scent consumes the findings of every producer agent in the pack — all running against the same local navi.db — and renders them as one ranked, tiled exposure view. It's a reader, not a writer: the view changes when the estate does.

WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

  • One page, whole pack — the correlation payoff of fifteen specialists without opening fifteen tools.
  • Built for the morning glance — ranked and tiled for a daily read, not another dashboard nobody opens.
  • Honest by inheritance — every number on the page carries its source agent's evidence and coverage caveats.

PROOF POINTS

  • In a reference environment of 268 assets, one page rolls up:
  • 2,631 KEV findings (Laelaps) · 63 reachable attack paths (Fenrir) · 44 AI assets (Pythia) · ~100% quantum-vulnerable certs (Heimdall) — each tile one click from its evidence.

Illustrative demo-lab context — not a guarantee. The view is only as complete as scan coverage; blind and uncredentialed hosts are called out, not hidden.

WHO IT'S FOR

CISOs who need the estate on one page; vulnerability management leads running the morning stand-up; anyone building executive reporting that survives questioning.
